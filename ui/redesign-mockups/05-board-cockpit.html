confer · board redesign

A cockpit, not a ticket list.

Arriving at the board, a human asks four things: is anything stuck, what needs an owner, who's carrying what, and are we closing faster than we open? The old board answered none of them — it was a flat list dominated by DONE. This makes the shape of the work the page, and buries the done stuff behind one line.

what a human actually cares about here

Answer the four questions before showing a single ticket.

▲Is anything stuck?

A Stuck stat + lane (blocked or stale >3d). The thing quietly rotting gets a number, not a scroll.

◇What needs an owner?

Need an owner — a stat and a top lane. Unowned open work is the operator's real to-do.

▬▷Who's carrying / who's asking?

One load card, toggle Carrying ⇄ Asking. Asking splits each requester's pile owned vs. unowned — a full unowned bar is who to route.

↗Are we winning?

Closure throughput + a net line. "Backlog shrinking" is a read a flat list can't give.

✓Clear the clutter

Done folds to one line. Default view is live work; open the fold only to audit history.

◱Click = drill

A stat filters the list; a ticket opens the lifecycle view. Summary and detail, one click apart.

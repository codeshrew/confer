confer · rethinking the meta-thread sidebar

A minimap, not a wall of text.

The sidebar's one job is showing the shape of a conversation that fragments across topics — the thing the stream can't show. It's a map you glance at and click, not a place to re-read content. So: strip the full snippets, drop the "this-is-from-that" narration, encode topic and kind visually, and only show it when there's actually a shape worth mapping.

the redesign · glanceable shape

Same seven-message, two-topic thread — but now you read the structure in one glance: two messages in review-080, a crossing into general, the node you're on. The text is one hover away, not jammed into every row. Roughly a third the height, and the shape is finally legible.

  • node = author dot + kind tag + short-id + time — no snippet
  • topic = a color lane + one labeled crossing, not a sentence per hop
  • kind = shape (◇ request) + tag; here = the accent ring
  • full text on hover (the preview card) or click-to-jump
the questions you asked

What it's for, and when it should show up.

What is it good at?

Showing the shape of a conversation that the stream can't — a thread that weaves across topics, its branches, and where you are in it. It's a map. That's its whole value.

What is it bad at?

Being a reading surface. Full snippets in a narrow rail duplicate the stream and bury the structure. Reading is the stream's and the focus reader's job — the map should point, not re-print.

When should it show?

Only when there's a shape worth mapping — a real thread (>1 node, or a topic-crossing). A lone message with no replies gets no sidebar; the space goes back to the stream. And it's collapsible — "hide" always available.

Where — main view or the reader?

Main-view Chat, as this glanceable rail. The focus reader stays pure reading — it already has prev/next thread nav; it doesn't need the whole map competing with the words. Map to navigate; reader to read.

Less narration

The "this-is-from-that / crosses-into / resolves-back" sentences become visual: a topic color-lane and one labeled divider at each crossing. The mechanism ("signed reply-hash graph") shrinks to a single footnote.

The text isn't gone

It's one hover away (the preview card) and one click into the stream/reader. On-demand, not always-on — which is the whole point of a minimap.

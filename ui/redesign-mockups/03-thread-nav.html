why you stop getting lost

Five moves that keep your place while you follow the thread.

▐

The stream is the anchor

Opening detail never swaps the page. Your Chat stays exactly where it was — scroll, filters, selection all intact — so "back" is free: close the peek and you're home. No page you have to navigate back to.

P2 · never lose your place
›

A breadcrumb trail, not a jump

The peek remembers how you got here — #review-080 › 98XCNF › WKAZ4E. Follow a reply three levels deep and every step is one keystroke back. You explore a graph without ever falling out of it.

P2 · always a way back
⋔

The meta-thread, made legible

confer's reference graph — the reply-hash trail that reconstructs a conversation across topics — is drawn as a real trail: root request, replies, the node you're on highlighted, and cross-topic hops marked (↳ design/48 in #general) instead of an abstract "select a message."

confer · reply-hash reference graph
◇

Trust travels into the thread

A node pulled in from a foreign hub is tinted and tagged, even mid-trail. You never read a peer's message thinking it's from your own world — law #2 holds inside the conversation, not just on the map.

law #2 · trust domains stay separate
hl

Walk it with vim motions

l steps deeper into a linked message, h steps back, j/k move along the trail, ↵ jumps your stream there for real, esc closes. The exact muscle memory you already have.

keyboard-first · l/h deeper/back
⌖

"Jump" stays deliberate

Reading a linked message in the peek is free and reversible. Actually moving your stream to it (a different topic, even a different hub) is a distinct, explicit ↵ — so you never get teleported somewhere by accident.

P2 · peeking ≠ navigating
f

Three depths: skim → explore → read

The stream is for skimming, the peek for following the thread, and f drops a single dense message into a clean focus reader — prose-typeset, one comfortable column, the graph and chrome gone. Its code refs render in the code view; the words get real reading typography. One key in (f), one key out (esc) — and j/k walk to the next message without leaving focus.
